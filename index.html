Birdy Help

Common questions and answers

Why does Birdy need Screen Time access?

Birdy uses Apple’s Screen Time permission to block selected apps during focus sessions and scheduled sessions. This happens entirely on your device — Birdy does not see what apps you use or collect any usage data.

An app wasn’t blocked — why?

iOS may temporarily pause blocking after a device restart, system update, or if Screen Time permission was removed. Opening Birdy once usually restores blocking immediately.

My scheduled session didn’t start

This is almost always caused by Screen Time permission being turned off by iOS. Open Birdy and check permissions — once re-enabled, schedules will run automatically again.

I didn’t get a notification

Make sure notifications are allowed for Birdy in iOS Settings. Focus sessions still run even if notifications are disabled — you just won’t see the reminder.

How do App Limits work?

App Limits let you allow a certain number of opens per day. After that, Birdy gently blocks the app to help you stick to your intention.

How do Scheduled Sessions work?

You choose times when distractions should be blocked automatically. Birdy will run these sessions every day without needing you to remember.

How do breaks work?

You can pause a session anytime. When the break ends, Birdy resumes blocking automatically.

How do I restore my subscription?

Open Settings → Restore Purchases. This reconnects your Birdy Plus access if you changed device or reinstalled the app.

How do I cancel Birdy Plus?

Subscriptions are managed by Apple. Go to your iPhone Settings → Apple ID → Subscriptions → Birdy.

Does Birdy collect my data?

No. Birdy does not collect personal data, track usage, or store your activity. Everything happens locally on your device.
